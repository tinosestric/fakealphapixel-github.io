#fakealphapixel

First IRL Conceptual Art NFT project and 1/1 collection

What is it all about, and why it has value?

#fakealphapixel is the first Conceptual Art project, sourced from the topic of NFTs and their critics, exhibited virtually and physically in a renowned gallery.

My main goal is to make Art that questions the value of digital assets, that could go from 0 to ∞, and relation to their communities.

It all started with a genious idea of my friend prof. Antonio Rollo - minting a transparent pixel - the shortest possible .gif code of alphapixel on the blockchain. As I was analysing the scene at the time, and saw how the Art and Artists got exploited in early 2021, I immediately produced a response with deploying "declared fakes of alphapixel" (with artist's statements) on 8 blockchains, that i knew at the time supported codeless minting. I started to make site specific Art interventions that resulted with an exhibition in Galerija Gallery on the end of October 21. I also play with the semantic jokes, as a response to silly marketing and sales copys that are used in some cases. 4x4 #fakealphapixels - off road, is one example... The project is ongoing and i'm constantly adding new material to my collection, working on couple of exhibitions and web based projects, and meanwhile researching the web3 applications of my fake raw materials for windows in metaverses. I'm not prommising any value, but it formed by itself by recent purcases on opensea for couple of ETH.

Here are some artworks from the series #fakealphapixel with brief descriptions:

#fakealphapixel ON CANVASS

Artwork represents a painting with the printscreen of proof of burn the #fakealphapixel NFT on BSC network, with a transparent plastic foil pixel phisically burnt over it. The pedestal of installation is a distached wooden frame, packing paper - transport pakage in which the painting was brought to the exhibition, and a standing lighter, clearly an invite to phisically burn the Artwork. Unfortunaly noone had enough courage to do so.

#fakealphapixel ON PAPER

This installation is a pixel square hole, a cutout from crushed semi-transparent piece of paper. With looking through it viewers could read the words: "it's a hole" that were written by hand on the pedestal. It's ment to be an impulse to think about the reciprocity of digital assets, and their fooling appearance in context of a collector.

#fakealphapixel EAT

The installation was made from edible transparent cubes, that were assembled in composition of letters "Eat". Intention was to provoke the viewer to eat them, and it was succesfull. In a part of performance that I initiated, some of us tasted the fake transparent pixels from a decorative pedestal. It's setting was in horizontal position, to resemble the improvised snacks that are usually put on display on such exhibitions.

#fakealphapixel proof of transparency - pot

The artwork is a collection of qr-codes, that were leading to all the of listings on marketplaces, each on different blockchains, where #fakealphapixels were deployed. On the end there was a sticker claiming, that these are a proof of transparency for the whole project. They really were.

#fakealphapixel - banksy mode

This is the outcome of performance, where i gave a comment on the actions of people, that cut the Banksy's street art out of their original walls for whatever reasons. I performed a cut out of transparent pixels of my #alleged# street art piece, a mobile device glued on the wall, and put them on display in a gallery as the remaining layers of hardware. Photo documentation of the event was also presented in a culture jamming approach on a fake Time magazine cover.

#fakealphapixel 6d

This scultpure is inviting the viewer to give it a push and put the installation to it's crash - merge. Two cubes 2x3d = 6d. The top heavy plaster cube standing in ballance on it's corner, put in a manner that needs just a little push, to land on the lower fragile glass cube. Pedestal is depicting the shortest possible .gif code of a transparent pixel. I was assuming, that if there was too much visitors of the exhibition, someone had to make a mistake and cause the inballance. It'd be a catastrophy in their mind, but a nice depiction of crashing the blockchain in case of too many users.

copies.
